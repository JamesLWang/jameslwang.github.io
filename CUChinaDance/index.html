About Us



Founded in 2013, Columbia China Dance is the only dance group at Columbia University that focuses on traditional Chinese dance from around the country. Each year, the club performs a number of shows at prominent cultural events, such as Night Market, Lunar Gala, and the CU TASA Philanthropy banquet. The club holds practices and/or open workshops every Sunday and puts on an annual showcase towards the end of the year. Columbia China Dance hopes to share the beauty and diversity of Chinese culture with the community around it.

哥伦比亚大学舞韵社团（COLUMBIA CHINA DANCE）初次成立于2013年9月，2017年被正式认可为哥伦比亚大学的官方社团，是目前哥大校园内唯一的官方中国舞社团。

舞韵自创办以来，吸引了众多对于中国传统和舞蹈文化感兴趣的同学，主要以WEEKLY WORKSHOP和表演的形式将中国民族舞和古典舞在哥大校园以及纽约地区内推广。舞韵曾数次在 COLUMBIA UNIVERSITY NIGHT MARKET, CU TASA PHILANTHROPHY BANQUET, 哥伦比亚大学春晚，林肯中心高校联合春晚等大型活动的舞台上呈现高质量的精彩演出。2019年3月，在80余位演职人员的努力下，舞韵举办了自成立以来的第一届showcase - “镜花水月”主题的年度演出，不仅在演出当天座无虚席，还收到了纽约各界的广泛关注与好评。之后还受到多家媒体采访，包括人民网海外的两次报道。

舞韵的社员以优美的舞姿和气韵演绎出了中国舞蹈的刚柔并济之美，成功地将中华民族的传统文化与底蕴以表演艺术的形式展现给了海内外观众，赢得了广泛的赞誉。舞韵不仅是哥大春晚舞蹈类节目表演的主力，还是哥大全球中心-北京高度认可的社团。

除了演出任务，舞韵也是一个注重于多方面发展的艺术平台：舞团在每周日都会举办例行Open Workshop，报名即可参加（不仅限于表演队成员），旨在邀请更多感兴趣的同学体验舞蹈培训的魅力，同时也了解舞韵的点点滴滴。希望成员们可以通过舞蹈陶冶情操，以舞会友，怀揣着饱满的热情善于发现生活中的美。

舞韵每年秋季通过audition招募新表演队成员，通常每年有近30位成员，承接各类演出。

期待热爱舞蹈和传统文化的你加入舞韵大家庭！

Welcome to Columbia China Dance!

欢迎参访哥大舞韵！


Columbia China Dance seeks to spread awareness of and share the beauty of Chinese culture with the Columbia community and greater NYC area through the performance and outreach of authentic Chinese classical and folk dances.

舞韵旨在以表演中国古典舞和民族舞的形式将中国文化在哥大校园以及纽约地区推广。

Recent Updates

March 10, 2019

Columbia China Dance hosted its first ever showcase “Castle in the Air,”《镜花水月》, highlighting Chinese classical, folk, and contemporary style dances. Members from outside the Columbia community were invited to perform as well, including dancers from Asian Fusion Dance at NYU and Sunshine Dance. With the collaborative efforts of more than 80 performers and backstage workers, the showcase was a success, receiving extensive attention and praise from the greater NYC community. Columbia China Dance was also honored to have famous Chinese dancer, Lijun Zhou (周丽君), teach members her signature dance, Duex Belle (尤物). Following the performance, board members and Ms. Zhou was interviewed by many media and was featured twice by People’s Daily Online.

Team

Nikita Tang - President
 SEAS 2022, Financial Engineering Major, Computer Science Minor
• Nikita has been with Columbia China Dance since Fall of 2018
• Nikita practiced rhythmic gymnastics competitively for 10+ years and was a US National Elite Team Member. She started Chinese dance at age 16 and performed at many festivals, senior centers, and schools as well as won champion at national and international competitions.

Dance offers me something more than just the technical challenge found in rhythmic gymnastics; it includes an emotional and spiritual aspect that allows me to really feel myself and immerse myself into my surroundings. The way I feel when the music comes on and my body takes over is amazing and allows me to experience freedom from the stress and work of my daily life. Dance is more than just a hobby to me, it is a world where I feel belonged, a family where unbreakable bonds are created. Come join Columbia China Dance’s family!!!
Asteria Liu - Vice President and Publicity Chair
 Barnard 2022, Comparative Literature and Film Studies Major
• Asteria has been with Columbia China Dance since Fall of 2018
• Asteria practiced 6 years of Chinese Folk Dance, 5 years of Classical Ballet

Dancing is part of my life. It speaks out the unique beauty to the public through its variously artistic expressions and aesthetic appreciation. To me, dancing truly helps establish a wonderful balance between work and leisure. My time at Columbia China Dance not only enriches my college experience but also makes it possible for me to discover more people who share the same interest as I do.
Amber Zhao - Publicity Chair, Secretary
 SEAS 2021, Earth and Environmental Engineering, Premed
• Amber has been with Columbia China Dance since Spring 2019
• Amber practiced Chinese dance from age 4-16 with a number of dance theaters, and started dancing again at 20.

I always enjoyed Chinese Dance as a kid because it allowed me to connect with other kids my age who I shared a similar background with—something that was rare for me at the time. Now, I enjoy dancing because it allows me to connect with my culture and heritage, as well as share the beauty of Chinese culture with my community.
Olivia Gong - Treasurer
 Barnard 2022, Economics and Math Major, Chemistry Minor
• Olivia has been with Columbia China Dance since the Fall of 2018
• Olivia has been dancing since the age of 3 and over the years she has learned a variety of styles including Ballet, Jazz, Lyrical, Tap, and Chinese Dance.

I started dancing at a very young age and I was giving multiple opportunities to stop dancing throughout my childhood, but for some reason I always would return to it. Dance has always been there and I have made some of my longest lasting friendships through dancing. Dance has definitely become more than just movement or expression, it is a lifestyle that I have come to know, love, and miss—especially since starting college I have not been able to dance as much as I used to. Through Chinese dance I have been able to further embrace and learn about my cultural heritage as well as spread the beauty of Asian art within the community.
